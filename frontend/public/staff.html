Integrated press leadership

BU Press operates as a connected publishing system inside Babcock University, bringing together editorial direction, design support, and production coordination.

  • Publishing leadership anchored in academic communication.
  • Editorial review that strengthens manuscripts before production.
  • Graphics support that protects the Babcock brand.
Team

Core staff of Babcock University Press

These profiles are based on the legacy BU Press staff page and adapted into a cleaner card layout for the rebuilt site.

Director

Associate Professor Samuel Okere

Dr. Okere leads Babcock University Press and brings decades of publishing practice across the university press environment and the wider private publishing sector. His background combines publishing studies with communication scholarship.

Editor

Dr Margaret Solo-Anaeto

The editorial function at BU Press focuses on strengthening manuscripts so they are fit for purpose, academically sound, and ready for publication. Dr. Solo-Anaeto also contributes to teaching and research within Babcock University.

Head of Graphics

Mr Henry Egware

The graphics unit supports the visual presentation of BU Press output, from cover development to branded design execution. It also reinforces the broader Babcock visual identity in print and communication materials.

How the team works together

Publishing direction, editing, graphics, finishing, and print production are treated as linked stages rather than isolated tasks. That integrated model is one of the strongest ideas in the legacy BU Press source.

Director's office

For publishing direction, institutional projects, and formal press engagement, the Director's office remains the primary point of contact.
