For Authors

Clear submission expectations for authors, researchers, and academic units.

Professional presses explain what they publish, what they expect, and how authors should prepare. This page gives Babcock University Press that missing professional layer.

Babcock University Press / For Authors

Before you submit

The portal handles account creation and manuscript intake, but authors should know the editorial expectations before they begin.

  • Prepare a clear manuscript summary and full author information.
  • Match your project to the press's academic and institutional scope.
  • Use the portal only after your submission package is genuinely ready.
Publishing Scope

What BU Press is best placed to publish

This guidance reflects your current platform, your live publishing workflow, and the standards commonly presented by professional university presses.

Academic books and textbooks

Monographs, textbooks, reference works, and scholarly titles that support teaching, curriculum development, and research.

Journals and recurring research outputs

Periodicals and institutional scholarly publications that need a stable editorial and production path.

Institutional and knowledge projects

University-facing publication programs, commemorative titles, conference materials, and mission-aligned public knowledge work.

Submission Pathway

A clearer end-to-end process for prospective authors

Your portal already handles approvals, manuscript submissions, reviews, contracts, and publication stages. This page now explains that process publicly in a way authors can trust.

1

Create an author account

Register as an author with your academic identity, department, qualifications, expertise, and biography so the press can assess your publishing profile properly.

2

Wait for profile approval

Author access should be approved before the workspace becomes active. This protects editorial quality and ensures the press is working with verified contributors.

3

Submit a complete manuscript package

Provide title, category, abstract, description, manuscript file, keywords, and any notes that help the editorial team route the work correctly.

4

Move through review, contract, and production

The press can then review the work, request revisions, issue contracts, move accepted titles into production, and publish the final book to the public catalog.

Submission Package

What to prepare before opening the portal

  • A strong working title, category, and concise statement of audience and contribution.
  • An abstract or editorial summary that explains the argument, purpose, and significance of the work.
  • A clean manuscript file in an accepted format, with enough revision to support serious review.
  • Accurate author information, including faculty, department, expertise, and short biography.
  • Any notes on co-authors, prior editions, translations, permissions, or related institutional context.

Common reasons submissions slow down

Professional presses reduce delay by setting expectations early. These are the avoidable issues that usually weaken submissions.

  • Abstracts that do not clearly explain the contribution of the work.
  • Files uploaded before the manuscript is stable enough for editorial assessment.
  • Missing context on permissions, source material, or previous publication history.
  • Author profiles that are incomplete, unverified, or disconnected from the subject of the book.
Author Support

Questions authors usually need answered before submission

A standard university press site should answer these directly, instead of expecting authors to guess their way through the process.

Will BU Press accept revised or previously circulated work?

Possibly, but authors should explain the publication history, prior circulation, and what has materially changed since any earlier version.

Can departments submit institutional projects?

Yes. Departmental and faculty-led projects fit naturally within a university press environment, especially where there is a clear editorial owner and academic purpose.

When do contracts enter the process?

Contracts should follow positive editorial movement, not replace it. Once a title is accepted or clearly moving forward, the publishing office can formalize terms.

What if my manuscript includes third-party material?

Authors are still responsible for disclosing and helping resolve permissions for third-party text, figures, tables, and images. See the Rights & Permissions page for the formal route.
